Robert Trujillo Wife, Son, Family, Age, Height, Net Worth

Robert Trujillo is a songwriter and musician, best known for being part of the heavy metal band Metallica. The bass guitar player, who plays mostly finger, is famous for using the bass slap technique to give his audience an exciting and extended solo. He has since been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame along with all the other Metalica band members.

Robert Trujillo: Biography, Age

Robert Trujillo was born on October 23, 1964, in Santa Monica, California, but grew up in Culver City, California, where his father was a high school teacher. At a very young age, Trujillo fell in love with music, especially Jazz and Soul, which his mother loved to listen to at home. He eventually began to like his own sound after listening to jazz bassist Jaco Pastorius, who used funk, rock, soul and punk music.

In high school, Trujillo honed his skills. He played bass, went to many backyard and house parties, played musicians like Ozzy Osbourne and Led Zeppelin before enrolling in jazz school at age 19. years. His original intention was to become a session musician, but his passion for the sound he had fallen in love with took him in another direction.

In 1989 Robert Trujillo joined crossover thrash band Suicidal Tendencies also worked with funk metal supergroup Infectious Grooves. As part of Suicidal Tendencies, Trujillo has appeared in albums like Lights…Camera…Revolution! (1990), The Art of Rebellion (1992), Still Cyco After All These Years (1993), and Suicidal for Life (1994). Along with Infectious Grooves, which also had Suicidal Tendencies lead singer Mike Muir, Trujillo appeared on albums like The Plague That Moves Your Booty… These Are the Infectious Grooves (1991), Sarsippius’ Ark (1993), Family Groove Cyco (1994), andMas Borracho (1999).

In the late 1990s, Trujillo got down to business with his idol Ozzy Osbourne collaborating on several of his albums before joining heavy metal band Metallica in 2003. Trujillo appeared on albums like Death Magnetic ( 2008), Lulu (2011), Beyond the Magnetic (EP) (2011), and Wired… to self -destruct (2016). Other bands he has played and worked with include Black Label Society and Mass Mental?.
